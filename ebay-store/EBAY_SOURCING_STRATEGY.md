# eBay Sourcing Strategy — Demand-First Rule

## Core Rule

**Demand first, supply second.**

1. Find what is already selling well on eBay
2. Find a matching source on 1688.com
3. List it

Never start from 1688 supply ("what can I scrape?"). Always start from eBay demand ("what are buyers already buying?").

---

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 1 — Research eBay demand

1. Go to eBay.com and search for a general category (e.g. "hair clips", "silk scarf", "reading glasses")
2. Apply filters: **Sold Items** + **New** condition + sort by **Best Match**
3. Study the top results:
   - Title keywords and structure
   - Price range (note the sweet spot)
   - Number of sales (the more the better)
   - Main image style (white background? lifestyle?)
   - Key specs buyers care about (material, dimensions, set size, color options)
4. Focus on **unbranded/generic** products — anything with a brand logo is VeRO risk

### Step 2 — Find matching 1688 source

1. Search 1688.com for products matching the eBay top sellers in style + specs
2. Apply margin filter: CNY price × 3.0 / 7.2 ≥ $9.99 (our minimum)
3. Prefer suppliers with:
   - Clean product images (no Chinese watermarks)
   - No brand logos on the product itself
   - Good sales history on 1688

### Step 3 — List

1. Model the listing after the proven eBay seller:
   - Copy title keyword structure (not the title itself)
   - Match price point
   - Use similar image composition
2. Follow the standard eBay pipeline (import → CSV → upload)

---

## What NOT to do

| Wrong | Right |
|-------|-------|
| Scrape 1688 by category → hope it sells | Research eBay sold items → find 1688 match |
| List a product because it's available | List a product because it's proven to sell |
| Pick categories based on 1688 supply | Pick categories based on eBay demand |

---

## Category Scope

Any category is fair game as long as:
- Products sell well on eBay (confirmed via "Sold Items" filter)
- We can source matching products from 1688 at 3x+ markup
- Products are brand-safe (no logos, no brand names)
- Not on the prohibited list (power banks, Amazon-banned earphones, etc.)

There is no fixed category list — follow the demand wherever it leads.

---

## Why This Rule Exists

Every listing slot is valuable. New eBay accounts start at **10 items / $500 limit**. Wasting a slot on a product that doesn't sell delays account growth and delays lifting restrictions.

Demand-first sourcing means every listing has a proven market before we spend time importing, generating CSV, and uploading.
